The Catholic Church's Vatican office has clarified that the doctrine does not have the power to give sacramental blessings to gay unions as God "cannot bless sin".

The orthodoxy office was responding to a question if the Roman Church's clergy has the power to give blessings to unions of persons of the same sex.

According to the Catholic News Agency (CNA) on Monday, March 15, the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith (CDF) said the blessings were sacramental and must conform with God's creation designs.

"Blessings are sacramentals, and consequently, in order to conform with the nature of sacramentals, when a blessing is invoked on particular human relationships, it is necessary that what is blessed be objectively and positively ordered to receive and express the grace, according to the designs of God inscribed in creation and fully revealed by Christ the Lord,” read the congregations note in part. God “does not and cannot bless sin: He blesses sinful man, so that he may recognise that he is part of his plan of love and allow himself to be changed by him,” it further said.

The decree distinguished between the church’s welcoming and blessing of gay people, which it upheld, but not their relationships.

The Vatican holds that LGBTQ people must be treated with dignity and respect but that gay sex is “intrinsically disordered.”

As previously reported, Pope Francis said homosexuals are also children of God who need families and should be protected by legal provisions.

The Catholic Church's head made his mind known during a sit down for a documentary christened Francesco, which premiered on Wednesday, October 21.

"Homosexual people have the right to be in a family. They are children of God. What we have to have is a civil union law; that way, they are legally covered," he was quoted in CBS News.
