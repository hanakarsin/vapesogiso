Auditor-General Nancy Gathungu has unearthed a mileage fraud in parliament and disclosed three members received KSh 16 million in travel expenses all within a day.

Gathungu revealed the trio were paid irregularly as they received domestic travel, mileage claims and overseas travel funds indicating triple payments in a single day.

The chief auditor said the Parliamentary Service Commission did not explain why the legislators were paid twice or thrice for the same date of travel.

Gathungu noted that in some instances, the MPs were paid a total of KSh 5.2 million for domestic travel yet they were abroad and received foreign subsistence allowances.

“In the circumstances, the propriety of the expenditure of KSh 11.4 million and KSh 5.3 million included under domestic travel and subsistence in the statement of receipts and payments for the year ended June 30, 2020, could not be determined,” she said in a report tabled before parliament.

Lawmakers usually receive KSh 18,000 in weekly mileage allowances for a return trip per kilometre depending on the distance travelled.

Lawmakers from the farthest regions of Mandera, Lamu, Garissa Wajir, and Turkana are the biggest beneficiaries with more than KSh 1 million claims monthly.

Gathungu said PSC paid out a total of KSh 1.4 billion in the financial year ended June 2020 to MPs for domestic travel.

Kenya Prisons scam

Gathungu in an earlier report put the Kenya Prisons on the spot for paying out KSh 975.7 million to ghost suppliers.

The auditor-general said the huge amount was disbursed to unknown companies signalling the money could have been siphoned off from public coffers once again.

"The verification of the payment vouchers at the stations revealed various irregularities as suppliers who were paid at the headquarters were not known to the stations,” Gathungu said.
