Deputy President William Ruto and a host of other politicians have condoled with Amani National Congress boss Musalia Mudavadi following the demise of his mother Hannah Atsianzale.

Ruto who was in the company of MPs Rigathi Gachagua (Mathira), Didmus Barasa (Kimilili), senators Kipchumba Murkomen (Elgeyo Marakwet) and Cleophas Malala (Kakamega) visited Musalia at his Nairobi home.

The deputy president mourned Mama Hannah as a God-fearing and responsible woman who raised her children diligently.

"She was focused and an enthusiastic servant of the community who has left behind a rich legacy. Our commiserations to the Mudavadi family and their loved ones. Rest in peace," Ruto eulogised.

Hannah, aged 92 years, died on Monday, December, 28, at 5am while undergoing treatment.

Musalia confirmed in a statement that his mother passed away at The Nairobi Hospital where she had been admitted.

"On behalf of the entire Mudavadi family, I wish to announce that our family Matriarch, Mama Hannah Atsianzale Mudavadi, has gone to be with the Lord. She rested at 5.00am today at the Nairobi Hospital. She was 92 years old. As a family, we are devastated by this loss but are bearing it with grace. Please remember us in your prayers. The Lord gives and the Lord takes. May our dear Mum's soul RIP," wrote Mudavadi.

Hannah was the widow of Moses Mudamba Mudavadi who died in 1989.

Mudamba was a member of parliament representing Sabatia constituency and a Cabinet minister who served under the late president Daniel Moi.

The family will hold a requiem mass and a special service on Thursday, January 7 and January 8 respectively.

Mama Hannah will finally be laid to rest at her Mululu home, Sabatia sub-county in Vihiga county on on January 9.
