Watermelon: Malava MP Malulu Injendi Ditches Tanga Tanga for ANC after Short Spell in Handshake Team

 Posted on July 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  520 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- ANC announced Malava MP Malulu Injendi's defection in a statement issued on Wednesday, April 14

- Injendi had a short spell in the handshake team after meeting with Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga

- He, however, changed tune two weeks later claiming he was duped by Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya into meeting the former prime minister

- The lawmaker has been one of Deputy President William Ruto's key allies in Western Kenya and his defection is seen as a big a blow to the Tanga Tanga faction of Jubilee Party

Malava Member of Parliament Malulu Injendi has ditched Deputy President William Ruto's Tanga Tanga camp for Musalia Mudavadi's Amani National Congress (ANC) party as the season for political defections gains momentum.

The ANC party announced Injendi's defection in a statement issued on Wednesday, April 14.

"Hon Malulu Injendi returns home. Malava MP defects to Mudavadi's team from Tanga Tanga. Welcome!" ANC posted on its social media pages.

Injendi's short spell in handshake team

In December 2020, Injendi had a short spell in the handshake team after meeting with Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga.

The MP who was elected on a Jubilee Party ticket in the 2017 General Election had then vowed to support the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) after opposing it.

The lawmaker expressed his support for the 2020 constitutional amendment bill after a lengthy meeting with Raila and Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya at the former prime minister's office.

"I was pleased to receive Malava MP Malulu Injendi who informed me that he has had a change of heart and has decided to join the team promoting the BBI initiative. He was accompanied by Governor Oparanya. Karibu sana Malulu," Raila said.

Injendi's about-turn

The legislator, however, two weeks later ditched Raila for Ruto again claiming he was confused.

Injendi alleged Oparanya duped him into meeting Raila without consulting him.

"I want to tell Governor Oparanya and Raila; I never Intended to meet Raila. Oparanya, you never told me we were going to meet Raila. You took me, and we started talking about things I never understood. I wanted to talk about constituency issues, but you engaged me in things that I never understood. I denounce all that happened on that day," he said.

"You took me there, and you understand very well that I am a Christian; I had a handshake that made me start shaking hands until Raila thought that I was dancing to reggae," said Injendi amid laughter and cheers from the audience.
