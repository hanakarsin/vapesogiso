Nairobi evangelist Lucy Wangunjiri says she broke a childhood vow on her wedding day

 Posted on March 11, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  522 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Lucy revealed she had made a decision not to ever get married after she and her siblings experienced agonizing upbringing in the hands of their ruthless father

- She broke her vow to herself when she met her hubby in Buruburu where she was working as a maid after being chased from home

- It was not a smooth sail during the initial stage of her marriage but they sacrificed to enable them honour their commitment to each other

Popular Nairobi Evangelist and radio show host Lucy wa Ngunjiri recently celebrated her 25 years in marriage.

Wa Ngunjiri who runs Prayers Beyond Boundaries Ministries disclosed how her marriage faced challenges they almost discussed divorce with her husband Lameck Ngunjiri.

However, what many did not know is that when she went down the aisle in August 5, 1995, she broke a vow she had made to herself.

She revealed she had made a decision not to ever get married after she and her siblings experienced an agonising upbringing in the hands of their ruthless father.

Wa Ngunjiri grew up in Kigumo, Murang’a county and their then drunkard father battered their mother and tortured them with intent of denying them education.

She was chased away from the village when she was 15-years old and ended up working as a maid in Buruburu before she met her hubby.

It took Ngunjiri persistence and resilience to not only convince Lucy to get married to him but do it through a church wedding.

Their marriage started on a rocky path immediately after the honeymoon.

“When my husband and I got married, we discovered that we were complete opposites. We started arguing after honeymoon, and from there things were just up and down with no joy though our family and friends thought we lived in a bed of roses” she disclosed recently.

Though they loved each other, the problem was that they did not get along because of attitude and belief system.

“But we had made a vow, and we decided divorce was not an option. I refused to let go even in my pain and we got counseling that proved I had too much pride in me. I thought I was too good for Lameck. I thought I deserved something better and understanding than he was” she disclosed.

They sacrificed to enable them honour their commitment to each other but they struggled for 10 years.

They now have two children; Leon and Lyne.

Wa Ngunjiri has a preaching show on Sunday mornings on Kameme FM.
