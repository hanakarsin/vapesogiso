Harmonize reveals he paid Wasafi KSh 24 million to terminate his contract

 Posted on July 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  553 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

Sensational Tanzanian singer Harmonize has revealed that he was forced to pay millions of shillings for terminating his contract with Wasafi Classic Baiby label prematurely.

The star said he had to pay back Diamond Platinumz's owned label TSh 500 million, which is equivalent to KSh 24 million, so as to be allowed to walk away.

Speaking in an interview with Clouds FM on Thursday, October 24, afternoon, Harmonize revealed the contract he signed years ago while joining Wasafi stipulated that he had to pay the label the aforementioned amount should he ditch it before its maturity.

According to the star, Wasafi remained with the rights to all the songs he did under them and will only hand them to him upon completion of the KSh 24 million payment.

With that, it means he cannot perform or use songs he did while at WCB for his own benefit until he clears the payment.

The star revealed that he had already paid a bigger chunk of the amount and is about to complete the payment after which he will freely use his songs.

Harmonize stated that the agreement was reached amicably and that there was and there is no bad blood between him and Diamond.

''Actually I left peacefully. There was a problem that we could not solve, we tried different modes to find solutions in vain. I asked them to release me so that I can be independent and they agreed,'' he revealed.

The star added that he did not have the money then but had to sell some of his properties to raise part of it.

He said he struggled and paid a bigger part and is almost clearing the payment.

''They asked that we stick to what the contract initially stipulated. The contract stated I should pay them KSh 24 million in case I terminate the contract before maturity. I did not have the money so I sold some of my properties, I even sold some houses I was constructing to raise part of the cash. I paid and I'm still paying but will clear soon because the balance is not that huge,'' he said.

Harmonize wrapped up by stating he made sure to pay up to avoid beef with the other party as they made him the big star he is today.

His revelation came just months after he ditched Diamond's label citing irreconcilable differences.

His exit sparked mixed reactions online with many claiming his star would dim without WCB's help.

Others, however, held he made the right choice and urged that he would even topple his former boss to become the creme de la creme of East African music.
