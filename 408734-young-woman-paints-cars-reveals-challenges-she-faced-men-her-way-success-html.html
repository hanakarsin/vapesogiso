Modupe Olagunju: Young Woman Who Paints Cars for a Living Reveals Challenges She Faces from Men

 Posted on July 3, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  364 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- A young woman identified as Modupe Olagunju, who paints cars for a living, explained how she got into that line of work

- According to her, she learnt this line of work while in her third year in Junior Secondary School to please her mother

- She also mentioned facing challenges from men who usually want to date her instead

- At one point, her shop was set ablaze due to jealousy, and she had to relocate

Modupe Olagunju narrated how her mother urged her to learn car painting work and the challenges she faced on the job.

Modupe, who is based in Oyo state in Nigeria, said when she started, most people made fun of her because of her kind of work, while some encouraged her to put in her best.

While interviewing with the BBC News Yoruba, the talented woman said she accepted to learn the job to make her mother happy.

"I usually have challenges from men who will insist on dating me before patronising me," she said.

According to the lady painter, her shop was at one point razed down by jealous people who envied her success.

The mother of four urged women from her, saying that they can always start small and expand later.
