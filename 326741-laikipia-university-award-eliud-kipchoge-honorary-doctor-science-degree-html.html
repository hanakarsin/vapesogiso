Laikipia University to award Eliud Kipchoge honorary PhD

 Posted on July 28, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  492 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- The university's Senate decided to award Kipchoge following his outstanding performance in sports

- According to the VC, Kipchoge's performance has not only brought fame to himself and the country but has also inspired the entire humankind

- The athlete's Vienna challenge which saw him emerge as the first human to run a marathon under two hours was recognised globally

- He was recently named the Male Athlete of the Year 2019 by the International Association of Athletics Federations

- President Uhuru Kenyatta had also awarded him the Elder of the Order of the Golden Heart (EGH) during Mashujaa Day

Marathoner Eliud Kipchoge is set to be awarded a doctorate degree by Laikipia University during the institution's seventh graduation ceremony.

In an advert published in national dailies on Wednesday, November 27, the university said Kipchoge will be honoured with a Doctor of Science (Honoris Causa).

The ceremony is set to be convened at the institution's Graduation Square, main campus, on Friday, December 6.

The vice chancellor (VC) said the decision was approved by the university's Senate following Kipchoge's outstanding performance in the world of athletics.

"The University Senate in making this award noted that Eliud has made tremendrous contributions in the real of sports. The senate specifically recognises his most recent achievement of becoming the first human in history to run a sub two hour marathon.In a remarkable feat of endurance, Eliud delivered his "moon landing" moment by crossing the finish line in a mind blowing time of 1:59:40 in Vienna as he delivered on his pledge to prove that 'No Human is Limited'," the VC said.

According to the Oxford Dictionary, Honoris Causa is a degree given to a person as a sign of honour and respect, without them having to take an exam.

This comes hardly a week after the athlete was crowned the Male Athlete of the Year 2019 from the International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF).

"Laikipia University invites Kenyans and the international community to celebrate with us this outstanding and humble athlete who continues to give his best to inspire human kind," the university said.

During the Mashujaa Day celebration that was held at Mama Ngina Waterfront Park in Mombasa, Kipchoge was awarded the Elder of the Order of the Golden Heart (EGH) award.

The award, given to him by President Uhuru Kenyatta, is the highest honour a civilian can receive from the head of state.
