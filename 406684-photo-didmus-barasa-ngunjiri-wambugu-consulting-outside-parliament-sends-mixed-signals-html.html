Photo of Didmus Barasa, Ngunjiri Wambugu Consulting Outside Parliament Sends Mixed Signals

- Others said Barasa had become a very important asset since it looked like Wambugu was trying to get him to join Kieleweke

- The duo was spotted together after the Tanga Tanga politician announced his four-month break from national politics

A picture of Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa and his Nyeri Town counterpart Ngunjiri Wambugu consulting outside parliament buildings has bewildered many.

The picture emerged hot on the heels of Barasa's announcement in which he conveyed his plans to take a four-month sabbatical leave to focus on achieving the elusive Luhya unity.

Taking to his Facebook, Wambugu simply captioned the photo "consulting" and left many Kenyans guessing on the Tanga Tanga MP's next move.

Others speculated Barasa could be on a spying mission for Deputy President William Ruto in the Kieleweke faction.

A section of other Kenyans said the Kimilili lawmaker had seemingly become a very important asset as it looked like Wambugu was trying to convince him to join Kieleweke.

Here are some of the reactions:

Vincent Gachanja said:

"The detective has been deployed to carry out investigations on behalf of the hustler nation, he will be back soon with a comprehensive dossier".

Abas Njuguna wrote:

"The guy looks so confused and undecided. He is unsure of whether to accept your lies or not".

Daktari said:

"You guys are busy making Didmus think that he is something that he is not."

Hours before the consultation between the two lawmakers, Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi visited Barasa regarding his sabbatical leave from politics.

Sudi, during the courtesy visit to his office, said he implored the lawmaker to reduce his four months break to a lesser time.

"I paid a courtesy visit to my friend Hon Didmas Barasa, Kimilili MP in his office earlier today. My visit was purposely to share my opinion regarding his four months sabbatical leave away from politics. I however requested him to reduce the four months to a lesser time. Let's wait and see what he will decide," said Sudi.
