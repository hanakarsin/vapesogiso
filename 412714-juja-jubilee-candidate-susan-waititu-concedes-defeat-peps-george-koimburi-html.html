Juja: Jubilee Candidate Susan Waititu Concedes Defeat to PEP's George Koimburi

 Posted on April 11, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  431 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- George Koimburi Ndungu was declared the winner of the hotly contested Juja parliamentary by-election

- Susan Waititu who contested on Jubilee Party ticket rejected the results over alleged malpractice

- A day later, the late MP's wife changed the tune and congratulated Koimburi for the victory

Jubilee Party candidate for the just concluded Juja parliamentary seat Susan Waititu Wakape has conceded defeat a day after the official results were announced.

Susan, who is the wife of the late MP, Francis Waititu, had earlier vowed to reject the outcome of the polls over alleged malpractice.

She accused her main rival, George Koimburi, of the Empowerment Party (PEP's), of bribing voters and colluding with electoral officers to rig her out.

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) on Wednesday, May 19, declared Koimburi, the winner of the Juja parliamentary by-election after garnering 12, 159 votes against Susan's 5,746.

Barely a day after the results were announced, the Jubilee candidate has come out to concede defeat and congratulate the MP-elect for the win.

"I accept the results of the elections by the IEBC, which declared George Koimburi the winner; I also want to thank all of you who worked with me through this journey," she said.

Addressing journalists on Thursday, May 20, Susan said he accepted the defeat she got from his opponent Koimburi.

She also confirmed that she had cancelled her decision to appeal Koimburi's win over alleged malpractice and intimidation of voters during the election.

"For the sake of peace, we shall let the matter rest. We shall not lodge any case," she added.

On Wednesday, May 19, Jubilee Party leaders in Juja decried electoral malpractice in the mini-poll, vowing to reject the results should their candidate not be declared the winner.

While addressing the press on Wednesday, Susan, widow of former MP Francis Wakapee, raised vote-rigging claims and demanded a fresh by-election.

"I'm calling for votes counting because this is really sad, especially for a woman being unfairly treated. Some votes were left in Jomo Kenyatta and some in Gachororo. If anything, we are calling for another by-election," said Njeri.

The Juja parliamentary seat fell vacant after area MP Wakapee passed away.
