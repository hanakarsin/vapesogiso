What is a report?

A report is a specific form of writing organized around concisely identifying and examining issues, events, or findings that have happened in a physical sense. Some of these include events that have occurred within an organization or findings from research projects or investigations.

Reports generally involve presenting your investigation and analysis of information or an issue, recommending actions, and making proposals. There are two broad types of reports in business communication, they include;

  • Informal reports
  • Formal reports

Informal report

The purpose of an informal business report is to keep a wide variety of people informed about the company's engagements. The writer's intention should be to prepare the report as a presentation tool so that the reader would not have to scratch their brain for any further information related to the informal report.

The primary duty of the writer should be to look for possible information scattered here and there. He should then go through some reviews if necessary and, after collecting all the necessary data, choose an appropriate path to present that information.

Like many other report formats for business research, an informal business report largely depends on a memorandum that shows the structure. The memo should be placed right at the top of your page, if you are sending any hard copy or at the top of your word pad page if you are about to send a soft copy.

The body should be filled with valid factual information. This will make your report more appropriate and comprehensive. Although it is an informal report, the body should be well structured and adequately adjusted by multiple lines throughout the page.

The structure of an informal report

It is the responsibility of the presenter to put the information in an organized manner. The sentence division and distinction should be increased with multiple topics, and those topics should be put under headlines.

It is quite natural to possess multiple references and notes while writing an informal business report, but all these references should not be included inside the body. All the references and notes should be placed under the memorandum.

Formal report

A formal report intends to identify a problem and find the solution, much like a dissertation. The writer of a formal business report must have a crystal idea about the instructions that should be observed while presenting a report.

A thorough understanding of the entire context is essential to pierce through that particular issue and offer a solution that could be implemented by the organization's managers.

The references that the writer attaches should be collected from reliable journals, and they should not be less than seven. The researcher must remain cautious so that any irrelevant source does not get added to the list in the report.

The package of the formal business structure should consist of the following points;

  • Title page

Having looked at the above broad categories, it is worth mentioning that both formal and informal reports have sub-categories. They include;

  • Informational reports: They provide a summary of the information and data found on a particular topic.
  • Analytical reports: One of the types of reports in research methodology. In this kind of report, information is researched and collected, and then the report provides an analysis that leads to one or more recommendations.

Therefore, you can have an informal informational/analytical or a formal informational/analytical report.

1. Periodic report

A periodic report is a factual presentation of both the positive and negative aspects of the working process. Sometimes, the organizations' events and working processes need to be analyzed and presented at regular intervals to focus on the milestones achieved and challenges that popped up.

The researcher must be careful about the type of information that has been gathered for establishing the project. There is no limitation of the category of information used in the body, and there are a few categories that generally follow this format.

The current status, activities performed, and the activities that are not performed, the difficulties faced, and avoided in that particular period should be enlisted in the information bucket.

2. Inspection report

The inspection report writing format of a company is prepared to measure the company's actual performance or individual business. The companies' achievements would be analyzed along with activities that they have been putting in their business for long.

There are a few categories of inspection reports that generally help to understand the performance of the organization. These include site inspections report, quality inspection report, and building inspection report.

4. Progress report

A robust communication process that helps the coordination among the stakeholders forms the base of a business progress report of an organization. The primary step of initiating a progress business report is to count the objectives for doing this project.

The report should not skip the logical outline of the report. It must start with an executive summary consisting of the research findings that the reporter has collected from valid sources. The mandatory part of the structure is the subheadings that would define your report in brief.

The reporter should assemble the content and various inputs before they develop a progressing team. Each team member should get the assigned task based on the structure of the report and their expertise.

A secure communication network should grow among the team members to make the reporting process more comprehensive. The reporter needs to focus on writing reports pattern professionalism, as the report would carry his name.

5. Process reports

Process reports focus on work processes, such as approving weekly outlays, time-sheets, travel expenses, among other things. These reports explain how something is done or how something came about. It can be written in detailed step-by-step instructions or a story format with the instructions subtly threaded throughout the narrative.

Usually, a process report has no minimum or maximum length, but five paragraphs are the norm. Its structure is a follows;

  • Title
  • Introduction
  • Body paragraphs
  • Conclusion
