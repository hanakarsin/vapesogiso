COVID-19: Health CS Kagwe announces 80 new cases, tally rises to 99,162

 Posted on February 23, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  542 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- Some 26 more patients recovered from the disease on Sunday raising number of discharges to 82,350

- Though at a slow rate, the death toll hit 1,731, following the demise of three patients between Sartuday and Sunday

- The country is eagerly waiting for vaccines whose first consignment is expected next month or earlier, according to CS Kagwe

The number of COVID-19 cases in Kenya has increased by 80 to 99,162, the Ministry of Health (MoH) has announced.

Announcing the new cases on Sunday, January 17, Health Cabinet Secretary (CS) Mutahi Kagwe said the patients were identified following testing of 3,733 between Saturday and Sunday.

" The cumulative tests so far conducted are 1,125,679. From the cases, 62 are Kenyans while 18 are foreigners," said Kagwe.

Kagwe further confirmed recovery of 26 more COVID-19 patients in the country, pushing total to 82,350.

"24 recoveries were from the home-based care while two (2) were discharged from the hospital," he continued.

Unfortunately, the death toll continued to rise on Sunday following the demise of three more patients. This raised number of fatalities to 1,731.

County distribution of Sunday's cases

Out of the 80 cases recorded today, 62 were from Nairobi, Meru (four), Busia and Kwale (three each) while Kiambu had two.

The remaining cases were drawn from Kericho, Kilifi, Makueni, Mombasa, Nyandarua and Uasin Gishu which recorded a single case each.

Hospitalisation

Kagwe said there are 699 patients admitted in various health facilities countrywide, while 1,642 are on Home Based Isolation and Care.

Some 30 patients are in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU), 14 of whom are on ventilatory support and 13 on supplemental oxygen. Three are under observation.

Another 13 patients are separately on supplementary oxygen with 12 of them in the general wards. One patient is in the High Dependency Unit.

Vaccines

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported the CS said Kenya was set to receive the much-needed vaccines in February 2020, or earlier.

"We have engaged various institutions, notably GAVI, the organisation leading in the acquisition of vaccines expected in Kenya in February. However, it is even possible to get the vaccines in Kenya earlier than that. The arrangements being made by both public and private sector are likely to see the vaccine here before the February date given by GAVI. We have also said that will are not going to use only one type of vaccine. Our partners (those linked with Moderna, Pfizer, AstraZeneca and Chinese vaccines like Sinopham) are having a meeting today to discuss how these doses are going to reach Kenya," said Kagwe.

Situation globally

As of Sunday, January 17, at least 95,049,394 people had contracted the disease since its outbreak in Wuhan, China in late 2019.

Over 2,032,835 people had lost their lives to the scourge while 67,876,639 others had recovered.
