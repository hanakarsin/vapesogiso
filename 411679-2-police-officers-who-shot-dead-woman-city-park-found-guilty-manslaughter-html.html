2 Police Officers Who Shot Dead Woman at City Park Found Guilty of Manslaughter

- The High Court found police constables William Chirchir and Godfrey Kirui guilty of killings businesswoman Jane Wangui

- The two officers shot the woman dead in May 2018 while she was in her car with her nephew Bernard Chege who sustained serious injuries

- Judge Stella Mutuku ruled the law enforcers shot with the intent to kill as the woman and her counterpart did not pose any danger

Two police constables who shot businesswoman Jane Waiyaki in May 2018 have been found guilty of manslaughter.

Police constables William Chirchir and Godfrey Kirui shot dead the woman who was with her nephew Bernard Chege, who sustained severe injuries on the morning of May 20, 2018.

While making the ruling on Tuesday, May 11, High Court Judge Stella Mutuku said the two officers used extreme force despite the victim and her nephew posing no threat.

Mutuku said the duo had not also committed a crime, and the officers thus behaved hastily and shot with the intent to kill.

She also pointed out that the law enforcers did not fire in the air or at the tires to deflate them but instead shot at Waiyaki at her passenger seat.

"All of the officers were armed, and they should have behaved cautiously. I believe there were other ways to intercept the car, and they also broke the law requiring them to protect life," she said in the ruling.

11 rounds of ammunition

According to a police report, a county security officer reported the presence of the vehicle, which had tinted windows in the park.

Makadara police officers who were on patrol responded, and their knock on the vehicle's window went unanswered.

The report indicated that the car then sped off, prompting the two officers to fire 11 rounds of gunshots into the car.
