Joyful Kirinyaga governor Anne Waiguru has expressed gratefulness to God as she clocked 50 years today, Friday, April 16.

Taking to her official social media platforms, the first term county boss said that she was grateful to God for the far she had reached in life.

The first-term governor also attached CeCe Winans's hit song, Goodness of God along with her post as she thanked her family and friends for the support they had accorded her all along.

"Today I turn 50! I am most grateful to God for I have seen His goodness in the land of the living! I have seen Him in my joys and successes. And through my seasons of fires and in the darkest nights, He has been closer like no other. I am blessed to have wonderful family and friends and to be in good health. What a blessing to turn 50 today," Waiguru said.

Waiguru comes off as a strong-willed woman in political circles. However, those who have closely interacted with her term her as a down-to-earth leader who is both interactive and charming.

Waiguru's rise to prominence has been carved through years of relentless toil serving in various roles in economic and public policy.

She has received several accolades in her lifetime signifying a competent technocrat whose sheer drive and commitment are simply unmatched.

From a civil servant working behind the boardroom to an excellence role as a governor, nothing seems too hard to handle for the former Cabinet secretary.

The mother of three sons is married to city lawyer, Kamotho Waiganjo.

TUKO.co.ke understands that Waiguru kick-started her public service career in the Office of the President where she served as a technical assistance to the Public Service Reform Secretariat.

In 2011, Waiguru was nominated among the Top 40 Under 40 women. She was the sole public service nominee.

During the time Waiguru served as the Devolution and Planning CS, she launched several initiatives, including the Huduma Centre that streamlined Government services to Kenyans.

Before she resigned from her CS position, 36 stations stretched countrywide had offered services to more than five million people.
