Esther Passaris rebukes discrimination of LGBT, claims there are gay people in government

 Posted on April 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  393 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- The Women Rep said there was no need to discriminate against members of the LGBT

- Passaris added gays are all over, from the government to business spaces to family circles

- According to the politicians, Kenyans should accept homosexuals for who they are

Nairobi Women Representative Esther Passaris has urged Kenyans to stop discriminating against people who enjoy same sex relationships.

Passaris said it was not fair to judge and stigmatise them based on their sexual preferences.

Speaking in an interview with Radio Maisha on Monday, May 27, the vocal politician claimed there are many homosexuals in government offices, business spaces, family circles who Kenyans should learn to live with in harmony and accept them for who they are.

''I know a lot of gay people and I don't panic when I meet them. I have many gay friends, we even have gays in the government, in business environment, people know them but we do not talk about them. There is stigmatisation. I do not support criminalising same sex relationship and sending them to jail over the same for 14 years,'' she said.

The politician's sentiments came barely a fortnight after the High Court declined to decriminalise sections of the Penal Code which makes same sex in Kenya illegal.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke earlier, High Court Judges Roselyn Aburili, Chacha Mwita and John Mativo dismissed a case filed by Eric Gitari and other members of the LGBT community, which sought decriminalisation of sections 162 and 165 of the law.

This is not the first time Passaris has stood up for the rights of the LGBT.

The Nairobi Woman Rep was badly trolled recently by netizens after sharing a post supporting homosexuality in the country.

The MP in her post argued that there was nothing wrong for two consenting adults of same sex to engage in a relationship.
