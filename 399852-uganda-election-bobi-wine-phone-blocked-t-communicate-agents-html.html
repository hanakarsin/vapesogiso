Uganda presidential candidate Robert Kyagulanyi alias Bobi Wine has claimed the authorities have blocked his mobile phone.

The National Unity Party leader suspected the withdrawal of phone service was part of President Yoweri Museveni's plot to ensure he was not in communication with his poll agents.

Despite the latest tribulations, Bobi called on his supporters to remain vigilant throughout the vote counting exercise.

"Like my wife's, my phone has been blocked and I am unable to receive or make regular calls. I know this is to stop me from communicating to our agents and coordinators. I encourage you comrades to be vigilant as I try to devise ways of reaching out to you," he tweeted.

The politician's wife Barbie Kyagulanyi had also made similar allegations when she could not receive or make phone calls.

At first, Barbie thought it was the normal poor network coverage but the problem persisted prompting her to reach out to network providers.

"For a full hour, I engaged my phone service provider customer care desk and they have no explanation whatsoever for their withdrawal of direct calls from my phone. Disconnecting me from telephone services doesn't stop us from completing," she posted on January 13.

Bobi addressed the media after casting his vote and expressed confidence of victory even as he called for peace.

In a separate but related story, Uganda Electoral Commission through its chairperson Simon Byabakama cautioned politicians against running parallel tallying centres.

“I want to caution that no person is authorised to declare the outcome of the presidential election other than the commission. I urge stakeholders to desist from pursuing such a path,”said Byabakama.
